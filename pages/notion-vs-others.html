Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Notion and its competitors

Is Notion AI worth the extra $10/month?

If you write more than 1,000 words per week — emails, notes, drafts, content — the AI pays for itself in time saved. It's excellent for summarization, rewriting, and first drafts. However, if you only take quick notes, the free version of Notion (without AI) is already excellent.

Can Notion replace my project management tool (Asana, Trello, Monday)?

For most small to medium teams, yes. Notion's databases, kanban boards, calendar views, and timeline (Gantt) cover 90% of what Asana or Trello do. Large enterprises with complex workflows may still prefer dedicated PM tools, but Notion is a strong alternative at a lower price point.

Is Obsidian really free forever?

Obsidian itself is free on all platforms with no feature limits. The only paid features are Obsidian Sync ($4/mo) and Obsidian Publish ($8/mo). You can use it forever for free with local files and community plugins. This makes it the most affordable option for privacy-focused users.

Which tool has the best web clipper?

Evernote's web clipper is still the gold standard. It captures full articles, simplified views, bookmarks, screenshots, and PDFs with reliable formatting. Notion's clipper is solid for articles but less comprehensive. OneNote's clipper works well for simple pages. Obsidian relies on community extensions like MarkDownload.

Can I use Notion offline?

Notion's offline support is limited. You can view recently accessed pages offline on mobile, but you cannot create or edit without an internet connection. Desktop offline editing was introduced in beta in 2025 but is still not fully reliable. If offline access is critical, OneNote or Obsidian are better choices.

Which is better for students: Notion or OneNote?

OneNote wins for handwritten notes and free pricing. Notion wins for structured note-taking with databases, toggles, and templates. Many students use both — OneNote for lectures (handwriting + recording) and Notion for study guides, task tracking, and collaborative projects.

Does Evernote have AI now?

Evernote rolled out AI-powered search and note summaries in 2025 under its "AI Notes" beta feature. It can find notes by meaning (not just keywords) and generate brief summaries. However, it's less capable than Notion AI, which writes, rewrites, and drafts content inline.

Which tool is best for team knowledge bases?

Notion is the clear winner for team wikis and documentation. Its nested page structure, databases, and permission controls make it easy to build a company wiki that actually gets used. Obsidian Publish works for public documentation. OneNote and Evernote lack the structure needed for serious knowledge base use.
